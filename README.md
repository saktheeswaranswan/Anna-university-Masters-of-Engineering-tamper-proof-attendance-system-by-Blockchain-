## 1. Ph.D. Categories: Full-Time vs. Part-Time
Anna University offers two main modes of pursuing a Ph.D. Candidates must clearly select their category during the application process.
| Category | Description | Key Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| **Full-Time** | For scholars dedicating their entire time to research at the University Departments, Constituent Colleges, or approved affiliated centers. | • Must sign the attendance register on all working days.
• Includes candidates on sponsored projects (min 2 years funding) or National-level fellowships.
• Employed individuals must take official leave and be formally relieved to join. |
| **Part-Time** | For working professionals (academics or industry personnel) who want to pursue research alongside their jobs. | • Must submit a No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the current employer.
• Must attend coursework, DC meetings, and research presentations.
• Needs recognized facilities at their workplace (for industry candidates). |
## 2. Eligibility: PhD With or Without a Master's Degree?
| Route | Minimum Qualification | Academic Requirement | Program Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Normal PhD** | M.E. / M.Tech / M.S. (By Research) / M.Sc / MBA / M.Arch | 55% Marks or 5.5 CGPA (50% or 5.0 CGPA for SC/ST/PWD) | Regular Ph.D. |
| **Direct Route** | B.E. / B.Tech (Exceptional merit) | Top academic performance and entrance clearance | **M.S. (By Research) + Ph.D. Integrated** |
> ⚠️ **Note on Order of Study:** Qualifications must be acquired in the strict chronological order of studies: 10th → HSC/Diploma → UG → PG. Post Graduate degrees acquired through distance mode, weekend mode, or work-integrated programs in Engineering/Technology are generally **NOT** eligible for Ph.D. admission.

## 3. Step-by-Step: How to Apply for a Ph.D.
When the admission window opens (typically August for the January session and February for the July session), you must follow these exact steps through the Centre for Research (CFR) Portal:
| Step | Action Required | Details & Constraints |
|---|---|---|
| **Step 1** | **Choose a Supervisor** | • You *must* obtain the consent of a recognized Anna University Supervisor before applying.
• Applications without prior supervisor consent are summarily rejected.
• Supervisors can take a maximum of 4 new candidates per session. |
| **Step 2** | **Online Registration** | • Create an account on the CFR portal when admissions open.
• Fill in personal, educational, and employment details. |
| **Step 3** | **Pay Registration Fee** | • **General Category:** ₹1,000 + 18% GST
• **TN SC/ST/SCA:** ₹600 + 18% GST
• **Other State SC/ST:** ₹1,000 + 18% GST (Online payment only). |
| **Step 4** | **Submit Online & Print** | • Submit the form online with the digital approval/endorsement of your proposed supervisor.
• Download and print the completed application form. |
| **Step 5** | **Hard Copy Submission** | • Attach necessary documents (Certificates, NOC for part-time, AIU certificate for foreign degrees).
• Send the hard copy to the Centre for Research before the stipulated deadline. |
| **Step 6** | **Entrance Exam & Interview** | • **Written Test (70% Weightage):** Must score a minimum of 50% to qualify for the interview.
• **Interview (30% Weightage):** Discuss your research proposal with the selection panel. |
## 4. Duration & Extensions: The True Timeline
| Timeline Phase | Duration | Official Status | Fee Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Minimum Duration** | 3 Years | Mandatory minimum time required before thesis submission. | Normal Semester Fees |
| **Standard Maximum** | Up to 6 Years | The standard completion window. | Normal Semester Fees |
| **Grace Period** | 6 to 7 Years | Extension granted by the Director (Research) with Supervisor's recommendation. | **Double** Semester Fees |
| **Final Extension** | 7 to 7.5 Years | Special 6-month extension granted *only* by the Vice-Chancellor. | **Double** Semester Fees |
> 🛑 **The Cancellation Risk:** If you fail to submit the thesis within the maximum allowed extended time, your registration is cancelled automatically. You must apply for re-registration, which is why some scholars get stuck taking up to 10+ years to complete the degree.

## 6. Curriculum, Coursework, and Internal Marks
| Component | Description | Evaluation Method |
|---|---|---|
| **Research Methodology** | Mandatory foundational subject for all scholars. | Internal Assessment (Tests/Assignments) + End Semester Exam. |
| **PG-Level Courses** | Minimum of 3 to 4 PG-level subjects related to your research. | Internal Assessment + End Semester Exam (Minimum 55% required). |
| **Doctoral Committee (DC)** | A panel of experts reviewing progress every 6 to 12 months. | First DC meeting approves coursework; subsequent meetings review progress. |
| **Comprehensive Exam** | Evaluates comprehensive knowledge after coursework completion. | Pass / Fail (Assessed by the DC). |
## 7. Attendance and Logbook Rules
| Requirement | Official Rule | Practical Reality |
|---|---|---|
| **Attendance (Full-Time)** | 100% expected on all working days. | Scholars sign a daily physical register at the research centre. |
| **Allowed Leave** | 30 days per year (max 15 days per semester). | Ensures consistent research progress. |
| **Logbook** | Mandatory daily/weekly research journal. | Must be signed by the Supervisor regularly and verified by the DC. |
